Downloadable Data Files

Data files from a variety of California Department of Education data collections are available to educators, researchers, and the public. Data files can be used to compare educational data with other data sets.

The "Link to Download Page" link will direct you to a Web page where the data file can be downloaded, along with information on the file layout of the downloadable file.

School Listing

Link to Download Page Description Years Available
Public Schools and Districts School code, district type, school type, grade span, county name, district name, school name, mailing and street addresses, legislative data pertaining to school, geocoding data, and other data for California public and charter schools. Current
Private Schools Addresses, phone numbers, school administrators, grade span, programs offered, and enrollment by grade for California's private schools. 1999 to Current

School Performance

Link to Download Page Description Years Available
Academic Performance Index (API) State accountability system includes Base API and Growth API school performance data. 1999 to Current
Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) Accountability system mandated by federal No Child Left Behind Act of 2001. 2002 to Current
Program Improvement (PI) Title I Program Improvement status based on Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP). 2004 to Current

Statewide Student Assessments

Link to Download Page Description Years Available
California English Language Development Test (CELDT) Students in kindergarten through grade twelve whose home language is not English are required by law to take an English skills test. In California, the test is called the CELDT. 2001 to Current
California High School Exit Exam (CAHSEE) Beginning in 2006, all California high school students were required to pass a test to earn a high school diploma. The test is called the CAHSEE. Senate Bill 172, signed into law effective January 1, 2016, suspended the CAHSEE diploma requirement and the administration of the CAHSEE through the 2017-18 school year. 2002 to 2015
Physical Fitness Test (PFT) The physical fitness test (PFT) for students in California schools is the FITNESSGRAM. The main goal of the test is to help students in starting life-long habits of regular physical activity. 1999 to Current
Standardized Testing and Reporting (STAR) Each spring, students in grades two through eleven take STAR tests. The STAR Program looks at how well schools and students are performing. Students take tests in math, reading, writing, science, and history. 1998 to Current

Demographics

Link to Download Page Description Years Available
English Learners English learners by grade and language. 1980 to Current
English Learner Staff Staff providing English language development, specially designed academic instruction in English (SDAIE), and primary language instruction and support to English learners. 1989 to Current
Fluent-English-Proficient (FEP) FEP students by grade and language. 1980 to Current
Graduates High school graduate data by race/ethnicity and gender, including total graduates, and number of graduates meeting University of California (UC) and California State University (CSU) entrance requirements. 1992 to Current
Graduates Meeting UC/CSU Entrance Requirements High school graduates meeting University of California (UC) and California State University (CSU) entrance requirements by race/ethnicity. 1984 to Current
School Enrollment School enrollments by race/ethnicity, gender, and grade. 1993 to Current
School Staff Demographics School staff by race/ethnicity, gender, education level, and years of service for teachers, administrators, and pupil services. 1997 to Current
Staff Assignment and Course Data School staff and course assignments for teaching, administrative, and pupil services staff, including course enrollment, grade level, highly qualified status, and time base. 1997 to Current
Student Dropouts Grade seven through twelve dropouts by race/ethnicity and gender. 1991 to Current
